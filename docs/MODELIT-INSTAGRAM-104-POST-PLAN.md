## Instagram Best Practices Built Into This Plan

### 1. **Optimal Post Format**
- **First 125 characters are critical** (shown before "...more")
- Hook readers immediately with value proposition
- Use line breaks for readability
- Include emoji strategically (1-3 per post)
- Keep hashtags at the end (separate from main content)

### 2. **Hashtag Strategy**
- Use 20-30 hashtags per post
- Mix of:
  - **Broad reach**: #teachersofinstagram #teacherlife #education
  - **Niche specific**: #systemsthinking #STEMeducation #scienceteacher
  - **Community**: #teachersfollowteachers #iteachtoo #edchat
  - **Branded**: #ModelITK12 #InteractiveLearning

### 3. **Posting Times** (Optimal for Teacher Audience)
- **Primary**: Sunday evenings (6-9 PM) - planning for the week
- **Secondary**: Wednesday evenings (6-9 PM) - mid-week inspiration
- Teachers engage most outside school hours

### 4. **Engagement Tactics**
- End with question or prompt (implicit CTA)
- Respond to all comments within 24 hours
- Use Instagram Stories to repurpose posts
- Tag relevant educational accounts when appropriate

## Implementation Workflow

### **Phase 1: Setup (Week 0)**
**Time Investment**: 4-6 hours

1. **Create Google Drive structure** (30 min)
   - Set up 52 week folders
   - Create _Master_Assets folder
   - Upload brand guidelines

2. **Design image templates** (2-3 hours)
   - Create 4 Canva master templates OR
   - Develop AI generation prompts
   - Test and refine visual style

3. **Build content calendar tracker** (1 hour)
   - Set up Google Sheets with all 104 posts
   - Add formulas for auto-dating
   - Create theme rotation schedule

4. **Write first 4 posts** (1 hour)
   - Test the template structure
   - Refine voice and messaging
   - Get approval on style

### **Phase 2: Batch Content Creation**
**Recommended Approach**: Create in batches of 12-16 posts (6-8 weeks at a time)

**Batch Creation Process** (2-3 hours per batch):

1. **Brainstorm topics** (30 min)
   - Select themes for this batch
   - List specific pain points/benefits
   - Draft angles for each post

2. **Write captions** (1 hour)
   - Use template structure
   - Ensure 3-sentence format
   - Add links and CTAs
   - Vary hashtag combinations

3. **Generate images** (45 min)
   - Use Canva templates OR AI generation
   - Maintain brand consistency
   - Export at 1080√ó1080px
   - Name files consistently

4. **Upload to Google Drive** (15 min)
   - Place in appropriate week folders
   - Update content calendar tracker
   - Mark batch as "Ready to Post"

**Suggested Batching Schedule**:
- **Batch 1**: Posts 1-16 (Weeks 1-8) - Create in December
- **Batch 2**: Posts 17-32 (Weeks 9-16) - Create in early January
- **Batch 3**: Posts 33-48 (Weeks 17-24) - Create in late February
- **Batch 4**: Posts 49-64 (Weeks 25-32) - Create in early May
- **Batch 5**: Posts 65-80 (Weeks 33-40) - Create in late June
- **Batch 6**: Posts 81-96 (Weeks 41-48) - Create in early September
- **Batch 7**: Posts 97-104 (Weeks 49-52) - Create in early November

### **Phase 3: Scheduling & Posting**

**Option A: Manual Posting** (Free)
- Use Instagram app
- Schedule posts 2x/week in your calendar
- Post at optimal times (Sunday & Wednesday evenings)
- Respond to comments throughout the week

**Option B: Instagram Scheduling Tools** (Recommended)

**Weekly Tasks** (30 min/week):

## FAQ & Troubleshooting

### **Q: How do I write 104 posts without sounding repetitive?**
**A**: The 4-theme rotation keeps it fresh. Plus, each theme has 26+ subtopics. Think of it as teaching the same concept 4 different ways ‚Äì teachers do this daily!

### **Q: What if I run out of time to create content?**
**A**: The batch approach gives you buffer. Create posts 6-8 weeks ahead. If you get busy, you have scheduled content ready to go.

### **Q: How do I measure ROI on this effort?**
**A**: Track:
- Website traffic from Instagram (Google Analytics)
- TPT store visits from Instagram (TPT analytics)
- Email sign-ups attributed to Instagram
- Community size (followers who engage regularly)

### **Q: What about Instagram Stories, Reels, and other formats?**
**A**: Start with posts. Once in rhythm, repurpose top posts into:
- **Stories**: Screenshot posts, add text, share testimonials
- **Reels**: Screen recordings of ModelIT in action
- **Guides**: Compile related posts into themed guides

### **Q: Should I run ads?**
**A**: Not yet. Build organic following first (6 months), then consider boosting top-performing posts ($5-10/post) to reach more teachers in your target demographics.
